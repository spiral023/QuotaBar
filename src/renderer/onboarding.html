What you can do with QuotaBar
Live Monitoring
Track session, 5-hour, and weekly limits in real time — the tray icon uses traffic-light colors to show how full your quota is.
Pace & Burn-Rate
See whether you're burning quota faster than the window allows — including a forecast of when your quota is likely to run out.
Cost & ROI
See what your usage would cost via the API and compare it against your subscription price — the ROI factor shows how much value you're getting.
Analytics & Models
Analyze usage trends, activity patterns, and model statistics: which models cost the most, how effective is the cache?
Notifications
Get alerts before things get tight: high utilization, quota resets, burn-rate spikes, or fresh quotas — all configurable.
History & Reports
Analyze historical usage over custom time ranges as tables and charts — and export reports as JSON.
Important for your history
Providers delete old logs over time
On first launch you only see what each provider still has stored locally — for Claude that's the last 30 days by default (setting cleanupPeriodDays; set e.g. "cleanupPeriodDays": 1095 in ~/.claude/settings.json for ~3 years). Codex currently retains all sessions. QuotaBar permanently stores every captured day and automatically backfills missed days on next start — as long as you run it regularly, your history grows without gaps even if a provider cleans up.
Start QuotaBar automatically with Windows
QuotaBar will then run quietly in the taskbar — click the icon to open the dashboard.
